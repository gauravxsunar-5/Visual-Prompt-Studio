## Prompt 1  
**Theme:** Art as Divine Power  
**Period:** Ancient Egypt  
**Artwork Inspiration:** Khafre with the Falcon God Horus  

A monumental ancient Egyptian stone sculpture of a seated pharaoh carved from dark diorite, exhibiting rigid frontal symmetry and idealized proportions. The figure sits upright on a throne with clenched fists resting on the knees, conveying stability and permanence. Behind the ruler’s head, a falcon deity wraps its wings protectively, symbolizing divine authority and protection. The surface is smooth and polished, with minimal texture variation, emphasizing timelessness. The composition is highly balanced and symmetrical, with no movement or emotional expression, reinforcing eternal stillness.  

This artwork reflects the belief that the pharaoh is both a political leader and a divine being. It was created to project power, reinforce social hierarchy, and ensure the ruler’s eternal presence in both life and the afterlife.

## Prompt 2  
**Theme:** Humanism and the Move Toward Science  
**Period:** Early Renaissance  
**Artwork Inspiration:** Giotto, Lamentation  

A fresco painting depicting a biblical mourning scene set within a naturalistic landscape. Figures are arranged diagonally, guiding the viewer’s eye toward the central emotional focus. The human forms are realistic and volumetric, modeled with light and shadow to create depth. Facial expressions and gestures show strong emotion and individuality. Soft earthy colors and a blue sky create depth and atmosphere. Overlapping figures and early perspective techniques enhance spatial realism.  

This artwork reflects the rise of humanism, emphasizing emotional experience and observation of the natural world. It was created to make religious stories more relatable and human-centered.
